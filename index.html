Prevalence of Mental Health Challenges among Physicians in Canada and Impacts of Poor Mental Health of Physicians on the Delivery of Patient Care

Introduction

OBJECTIVES

The study aims to determine the prevalence of mental health challenges among physicians in Canada, identify contributing factors, assess the impact on patient care outcomes and economic costs, evaluate existing support systems, and develop recommendations to enhance mental health support for physicians.

STATISTICAL ANALYSIS

According to the Mental Health Commission of Canada, the report, developed in collaboration with the Centre for Studies in Primary Care at Queen’s University and HEC Montréal’s Healthcare Management Hub, reveals 40 per cent of health-care workers are burned out, 50 per cent intend to leave the profession, and just 60 per cent are satisfied with the quality of care they provide

RESULT

There has been a significant increase in mental health challenges among Canadian physicians during the COVID-19 pandemic. This underscores the urgent need for systemic changes and enhanced support mechanisms within healthcare settings.

INTERVIEW FINDINGS

Physicians encounter emotional challenges when dealing with terminally ill patients, frustration with patient compliance, worklife balance issues, privacy concerns, feelings of isolation, and the impact of burnout on patient care.

RECOMMENDATIONS

Mental Health Resources for Physicians

The Physician Wellness Hub, provided by the Canadian Medical Association (CMA), is a valuable resource for supporting physicians' mental well-being. It includes articles on stress management, work-life balance, coping strategies, and mental health support. The platform offers self-assessment tools, peer support networks, and dedicated mental health resources tailored to physicians' needs.

Team-Based Primary Care Initiatives

The Canadian Medical Association (CMA) advocates a comprehensive approach to address Canada's healthcare challenges, emphasizing the need for team-based primary care. Their recommendations include easing the process for internationally trained physicians to get licensed, integrating various healthcare professionals into primary care teams, and advocating for a national health human resources strategy. Additionally, the CMA emphasizes the importance of reducing medical administrative burdens and promoting health data sharing across jurisdictions.

Physician Burnout

The Ontario Medical Association (OMA) has identified critical system-level solutions to address physician burnout, recognizing its significant impact on physicians, patients, and the healthcare system. The OMA Burnout Task Force suggests comprehensive measures such as streamlining documentation, ensuring fair compensation, promoting work-life balance, integrating telemedicine, and providing institutional support for physician wellness. These strategies aim to enhance physician well-being, improve patient outcomes, and optimize the overall performance of the healthcare system.
